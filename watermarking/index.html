What Is Document Watermarking?

A simple, proven approach to making every document traceable.

Available Provider

Aquamark offers document watermarking built specifically for the alternative finance industry, including email-based automation and API integration for CRMs and portals. Aquamark administers the Secure Funder™ program, but use of Aquamark products is not required for certification.

How It Works

When a broker submits a deal — bank statements, credit applications, tax returns — the documents are automatically watermarked before anyone at the funding company handles them. Each watermark includes identifying information such as the funder's name, the partner or broker who submitted the deal, and a timestamp. The result is a document that is fully usable for underwriting but visibly traceable to its origin.

This serves two purposes. First, it acts as a visible deterrent: personnel who know that every file they touch carries their organization's name are far less likely to redistribute it. Second, if a document does resurface outside the organization — repackaged in another deal or sold to a competitor — the watermark identifies exactly where the leak occurred and which submission it came from.

For brokers and partners, nothing changes. They continue submitting deals the same way they always have. The watermarking happens on the funder's side, automatically, before processing staff ever see the files.

How It Works in Practice

For Secure Funder™ certification, watermarking can be applied at different points depending on how your organization receives and processes submissions:

1

Email Submissions

Inbound emails containing broker submissions trigger an automated process that applies watermarks to attachments before they reach processing personnel. The broker's workflow is unchanged — they continue emailing deals as usual.

2

CRM / Portal Downloads

Watermarks are applied when files enter the system or at the point of download. Personnel who access documents receive copies that are already marked, so there's no additional step in their workflow.

3

Third-Party / BPO Access

If outsourced teams access merchant documents, watermarking ensures that every file they handle is traceable — reducing exposure without requiring changes to their existing tools or processes.

Why It Matters

In the alternative finance industry, the same bank statement can pass through multiple organizations during a single deal cycle. Without any form of protection, there is no way to identify where a leaked document originated. This makes it easy for bad actors to redistribute merchant data without accountability — a practice commonly known as "backdooring."

Watermarking doesn't prevent someone from viewing a document. It makes the document traceable, which changes the risk calculus for anyone considering misuse. When personnel know that files carry identifying marks, the incentive to mishandle them drops significantly.

Key Characteristics

Watermarks are applied automatically — no manual steps required from processing personnel
Compatible with OCR and fraud detection tools — watermarking does not interfere with downstream processing
No file storage required — documents are processed and returned without being retained by the watermarking provider
Can be integrated via API or email automation depending on your intake workflow

Certification Context

Within the Secure Funder™ requirements, watermarking is one of two accepted options for protecting accessible documents. The alternative is restricting files to view-only access with no download capability. Organizations may choose whichever approach fits their operational workflow — or use both in different areas of their process.

Any document watermarking solution that meets the described criteria is acceptable for certification. The program does not require a specific vendor or product.
